Estimated Civil Service Tea Consumption

Step 1: Get the latest total number of civil servants.

According to the Office for National Statistics (ONS), the total number of civil servants as of the 17 December 2024 release is 548,405. The next update is expected on 20 March 2025, so this value could be revised soon.

Step 2: Find the average fraction of tea drinkers and how many cups per day they drink.

  • 98% of Brits drink tea daily.
  • The distribution of tea consumption during working hours:
    • Consumption Group Percentage Estimated Cups
    29% drink two cups 29% 2 cups
    24% drink three cups 24% 3 cups
    6% drink more than five cups (assumed to be 6) 6% 6 cups
    Remaining 41% (assumed to be 3 cups) 41% 3 cups

    Weighted Average Calculation:

    (0.29 × 2) + (0.24 × 3) + (0.06 × 6) + (0.41 × 3) = 2.89 cups/person/day

    Factoring in tea outside of work (morning, evening), it’s reasonable to estimate a total of 3.5 cups/person/day.

Step 3: Final Estimate:

Multiply the total number of civil servants by the fraction of tea drinkers and the average cups per tea drinker:

548,405 × 0.98 × 3.5 ≈ 1,881,029 cups/day

✅ Estimated daily tea consumption by UK civil servants: Approximately 1.88 million cups/day
